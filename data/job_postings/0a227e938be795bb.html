Research Associate - Data Science - Washington, DC

:

IDA Science and Technology Policy Institute (STPI) is a non-profit, federally funded research and development center that provides analytical support to OSTP and other Federal agencies on S&T policy issues.

STPI is seeking a Research Associate (RA) who will contribute computational data analytics as a member of science & technology (S&T) policy project teams. The RA will implement experimental designs, data collection, analyses, and report writing.

STPI offers a dynamic, collaborative, and interdisciplinary team-based working environment where data and evidence inform Federal policy-makers and S&T policy. Research task timeframes range from same-day turn around to multiyear efforts. Responsibilities:

This research typically involves analyzing complex and often unstructured data; data visualization; social network and link analysis; statistics, including generalized linear models (GLM), longitudinal data analysis, survival analysis, hierarchical/mixed-effects models, structural equation modeling (SEM), and causal inference with observational data; qualitative analysis, including survey and interview data; basic natural language processing (NLP) over unstructured textual corpora. The successful candidate will support standards for data quality, workflow, and analysis; mentor colleagues; and engage with their professional community.

Qualifications:

The Research Associate must have:

  • A degree in data science or a related field (e.g., computer science, statistics, mathematics, cognitive science, cognitive psychology, education research, operations research) that includes completed courses in statistical analysis and modeling; MA/MS-equivalent degree or a combination of a BA/BS-equivalent degree and 3+ years related work experience
  • Demonstrated experience automating and optimizing reproducible data acquisition, extraction, cleaning, processing, analysis, and reporting
  • Demonstrated experience documenting and communicating research methods and results as well as the practical significance of analyses effectively using prose, tables, and data visualizations

Preferred experience:

  • Comfort with development using R and the tidyverse, ggplot2, RMarkdown, and/or Shiny
  • Experience, outside of coursework, analyzing messy data
  • Strong technical writing and presentation skills; ability to communicate with non-statisticians
  • Ability to balance commitments to multiple project teams simultaneously in a collaborative team environment
  • Ability to mentor and support others developing the above skills
