PCA Quality Assurance Reviewer #776 - Lawrence, MA 01843

Elder Services of the Merrimack Valley, Inc.


Position Responsibilities:

Quality Assurance: Assist in the development and maintenance of quality assurance systems and processes related to PCA Department functions. Track and measure PCA compliance with MassHealth standards and PCA regulations, consistent with the goals, vision and values of ESMV. Assist in the development of critical and analytical models to assess and improve customer satisfaction with PCA services. Work collaboratively with PCA Manager to develop training materials for staff, participate in the onboarding and orientation of new staff, and to lead re-trainings on an ongoing basis. Works as a member of the PCA team in cooperation with the PCA Program Manager to ensure smooth operations of the PCA Program and compliance with all EOHHS requirements.


QA Essential Functions: Core duties that are fundamental and not marginal to the performance of the job

  • Work with the PCA Manager to create and implement QA procedures and standards to measure and ensure skills training quality related to PCA program in accordance with standards for PCA set forth by MassHealth and ESMV
  • Assist Program Manager with Quality Assurance measures related to EOHHS PCA regulations by completion of newly opened and routine case reviews in cooperation with the Quality Assurance RN and Program Manager. Help ensure needed follow up and collect statistical data as required
  • Ensure timely follow-up by program staff to all NE Arc reports and requests regarding individual consumers
  • Act as liaison between ESMV and the MassHealth's third party administrator OPTUM. Ensure timely and accurate follow-up to all PAU requests by ESMV staff. Assist Program Manager in ensuring ESMV's compliance with PAU submission requirements through Quality Assurance measures
  • Assist Program Manager with compiling monthly MassHealth to SCO transfers
  • Perform the QA portion of the PCA annual employee performance review process by tracking and reporting relevant audit results and conducting audits as assigned
  • Monitor tools and reports to measure quality including compliance measurement reports and pre and post assessment systems for the PCA and Managed Care/PCA programs
  • Assist Program Manager with weekly MassHealth batch eligibility verification and suspension reports and ensure timely follow-up by program staff of all requests for suspension and resumptions due to loss of MassHealth
  • Assist with routine monthly billing reports by ensuring timely follow-up by program staff of all requests from Manager related to billing, submitting timely and accurate billing data to ESMV's fiscal department and collaborating with the Fiscal Department to ensure PCA quality standards are met
  • Assist with onboarding new staff and assume responsibility for essential trainings during orientation
  • Assist Program Manager with implementation, maintenance, and oversight of standard operating procedures for all Program staff. Collaborate to maintain the PCA Procedure manual and all in-house and public-facing materials to ensure accurate and organized information
  • Help to identify PCA department needs for training and education based on data gathered from quality audits and case reviews. Provide education and coaching to new and experienced PCA Skills Trainers and Administrative staff to improve processes or address performance issues according to established standards and systems
  • Participate in any identified peer groups and Mass Health directed meetings, trainings, and regulatory activities for purposes of meeting quality assurance requirements of Mass Health as required
  • Participate in professional development activities in order to be up to date with new and established Quality Assurance methods, trends and measurement activities
  • Other duties as assigned


Qualifications:

BA/BS in social work, human services or related field preferred. A minimum of 2 years Case Management or PCA Skills Trainer experience required. An Associates Degree with a minimum of 5 years Case Management or PCA Skills Trainer experience may be substituted for BA/BS. LSW or license eligible preferred. Required skills include excellent Quality Assurance ratings on past performance evaluations, strong detail orientation, excellent documentation skills, excellent interpersonal and communication skills with ability to effectively communicate on one-to-one level and with larger groups, ability to work independently or as part of a team, proficient in word processing, database entry, and e-mail applications.


Hours: 37.5 per week
