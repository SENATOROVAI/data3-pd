Yifei Yang: Medical Director in Clinical Chemistry and Toxicology

J. Warren Hunt: Group Manager, Chemistry II

Internship Description:

The fall internship project is designed to focus on evaluating and improving lab develop toxicology assays in clinical toxicology laboratories. The intern will explore laboratory data warehouse, linear regression models, and process optimization tools to support mass spectrometry based toxicology assays in the Chemistry Division.

Internship Goals:

  • Evaluate how LDT assays are established and maintained in a clinical laboratory setting
  • Evaluate the identification and reporting criteria required for quantitative assay
  • Present evaluation results within the chemistry laboratory upon completion

Knowledge, Skills & Techniques Learned from Internship:

The intern will gain knowledge of how to develop and evaluate a laboratory developed test and understand the necessary steps to ensure reporting reliable results. The informatics and data analysis will enable the intern to use statistical tools to find correlation, assign variables, and assess the relative effects of process optimization.

Measurement of New Skills:

Regular meetings with supervisor (mentors). The research components of the data would be presented in abstract, poster, and/or publication formats pending analysis results.
