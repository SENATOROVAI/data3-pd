What we do:
The Data & Statistics Function provides critical data capabilities in support of a safe and sound financial system. The Function manages the ingestion and quality assurance of financial and organizational data from domestic and foreign depository institutions, capital market participants, and bank holding companies.
Statistics serves as the front line to respondent institutions, engaging with them on a regular basis towards the provision of fit-for-purpose data for its stakeholders at the U.S. Treasury, the Board of Governors, and the Bank's Markets and Supervision Groups and the Research Function. These data are used regularly in the analytic support for the Bank’s responsibility related to monetary policy, banking supervision, payments systems and international market monitoring.
The Relationship and Regulatory Affairs Unit leads regular communication and connection with the industry, respondents, and key stakeholders, operating with a vision across the regulatory landscape and Federal Reserve System (FRS), to satisfy the Data & Statistics Function's mission of improved data quality at source and the creation of optimal stakeholder value. The team serves as point of contact for stakeholders for new data provider engagements, working collaboratively across business lines to ensure value of Data & Statistics offerings are well leveraged. In partnership with Data Collection & Analytical Services (DCAS) Department, this team provides quality assurance on issue resolution through navigation within the System and interpretation of policies and guidance.
Relationship and Regulatory Affairs is responsible for establishing and maintaining strong and effective engagement with financial institutions and industry groups to resolve complex issues, manage data designs and governance issues escalated from the Function, and communicate a macro view of industry themes.

Your role as Engagement Management Associate:
  • Utilize foundational concepts of area of discipline to drive the identification of broad industry themes in regards to data quality and data management and propose recommended initiatives to influence resolution or drive services of the Function
  • Develop and maintain close partnership with DCAS, Markets, and Supervision to assess respondent challenges and their remediation efforts; evaluating various aspects of firm’s governance framework, financial condition, operational resiliency, and systemic impact of efforts
  • Engage with industry groups to manage data designs and governance issues escalated from the D&S and to communicate a macro view of data management themes to drive improvement
  • Foster a culture of continuous improvement and advance the Function’s operational effectiveness by identifying opportunities to engage with industry and across Bank and System, and promote the growth of Statistics services and best practices in achieving Bank objectives
  • Leverages expertise to provide horizontal perspective to Department discussions, actively participate in initiatives of broader impact, e.g., working groups and committee meetings at local and national levels, on-site reviews, and escalates to Function items of note
  • Perform continuous monitoring and evaluation of regulatory report findings
  • Assist in the design, implementation and enhancement of policies, procedures, and controls for data management
  • Provide insight, industry best practices, and technical expertise to reporters

What we are looking for:
  • Bachelor's degree, preferably in Accounting, Business, Finance or Economics, with minimum 5 years relevant work experience
  • Strong communication skills and proven relationship management abilities to influence stakeholders and drive results
  • Strong analytical and decision-making abilities to lead engagement with and develop work products that inform data user needs
  • Ability to analyze the impact of new and evolving financial instruments, regulations, and market conditions with respect to promoting alignment across regulatory bodies (both domestic and global)
  • Generate and introduce new ideas in the area of data management and how Statistics services support those initiatives
