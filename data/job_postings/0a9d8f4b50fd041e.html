Position Summary:
The Electrical Engineer is responsible for development, strict adherence to and implementation of standards within the mill environment. As a part of corporate group, the Electrical Engineer will work closely with the Area Manufacturing Managers / Directors of the Corporate Electrical Reliability and Turbine Technology group to hone their skills as they become the future leaders of the ERTT group. Travel will be continuous for the foreseeable future as the Electrical Engineer gains the knowledge and confidence necessary to become the future leaders of the Corporate ERTT group.

Dimensions:
PCA is the fourth largest manufacturer of containerboard and corrugates packaging in United States. PCA is an ideas and solutions company. We are a leader in helping our customer package, transport and display products of all kinds. PCA embodies a culture of continuous improvement and, as such, is dedicated to delivering a high quality and sustainable product via innovative energy management and responsible design. In 2014, our 6 containerboard plants (“Mills”) produced 3.5 million tons of containerboard netting $4.5 billion in sales to third parties. Each of PCAs containerboard plants house a stock pulp machine, Fourdrinier machine, Presses, and a myriad of other containerboard equipment. In general, PCA strives for immediate response time in case of an emergency stoppage. The Electrical Engineer analyzes and plans the electrical supporting infrastructure for the mechanical and chemical processes to improve mill profits and decrease the plants losses.

Principle Accountabilities:
· Provide corporate guidance to Improve the mills profit and loss statement by executing plans to design, conduct and evaluate trial work to advance each of the mill’s economic situation continually improving safety, environmental, cost, quality, yield, productivity or compliance operations by improving PLC, Network infrastructure, and Data Acquisition at each facility.
· Evaluate long-term sustainability of all of the mills and make recommendations regarding equipment and control processes to continue a high level of sustainability.
· Investigate and solve production and process related problems by conducting studies and making recommendations to keep the mills effective in the task of safety and production.
· Communicates specifications and standards for the mills to follow as they relate to PLCs, Control Networks, and Data Acquisition.
· Support senior engineers and area managers in conducting quality and productivity studies, analysis and process change.
· When necessary, support the mills optimization/process control sustainability and continual improvement efforts by participating in environmental, quality, and productivity trials and assignments.
· Investigate methods to decrease waste, increase productivity, and supply solutions which improve quality and throughput.
· Work with senior engineers / Area Manufacturing Managers on system upgrades and new installations.
· Train mill personnel in all phases of project implementation.
· Creates I/O lists, cable schedules, AutoCAD drawing, CLX programming, and data acquisition programming.
· Know and follow policies and procedures of the company, share information that would benefit other members of the team and find ways to improve the delivery of service to our customers.
· Strive to continually strengthen PCA’s business philosophy of meeting needs and exceeding expectation for customers specifically supporting PCA’s Commitment to Service which involves doing what’s right for our customers, focusing on strategies that add value and support growth, and keeping the promises that we make.
Decision Making:
The Electrical Engineer is required to exercise discretion and creativity in determining best way to implement strategic projects that optimize processes. The Electrical Engineer is expected to drive performance of the team assigned to his/her project to meet project goals. The Electrical Engineer is expected to take general directions, and from those directions make decisions as to his/her approach to completing assigned projects. The Electrical Engineer is expected to determine how to operate and conduct assigned tasks within the established standards, procedures and cost constraints.
Interactions:
The Electrical Engineer interacts with manufacturing and support personnel of the mills as well as the corporate technology group to complete assigned work.
