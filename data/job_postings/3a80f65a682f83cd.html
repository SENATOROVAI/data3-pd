Facebook Reality Labs is dedicated to the research and development required to bring virtual and augmented reality to billions of people around the world. We have a neural data and signal processing internship available, working with our multidisciplinary Brain-Computer-Interface (BCI) team at Facebook's Menlo Park office, where we are developing novel wearable neural interface technologies. Our internship is twelve (12) to twenty-four (24) weeks long and we have various start dates throughout the year.
RESPONSIBILITIES
  • Apply state of the art methods for processing non-invasive neuroimaging data to develop state of the art brain-computer interface (BCI) applications for use in virtual and augmented reality systems
  • Analyze large and/or complex neuroimaging datasets
MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS
  • In the process of obtaining a PhD degree in neuroscience, biomedical engineering, , statistics, physics, electrical engineering, computer science, machine learning, or related discipline
  • 2+ years experience in processing and analyzing neuroimaging data, e.g. fMRI, diffuse optical tomography
  • 2+ years experience with a modern statistical programming language, e.g. Python, Julia, R
  • 2+ year experience with neuroimaging software tools, e.g. SPM, FreeSurfer, PyCortex Experience in applied signal processing and statistical analysis (e.g., multi-variate regression)
  • Must obtain work authorization in country of employment at the time of hire, and maintain ongoing work authorization during employment
PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS
  • Intent to return to degree-program after the completion of the internship/co-op
  • Experience with modern software tooling, including source control, unit testing, containers, and continuous integration
  • Experience in human neurophysiology measurement and human subjects research
  • Some hardware hacking skills
  • Experience with modern statistics and machine learning methods in neuroimaging (e.g.,time series modeling, bootstrapping, permutation tests, regularized regression, multi-voxel pattern analysis, graphical models, etc)
  • Experience modeling other neural data modalities, e.g. spike train, calcium imaging, EEG, MEG
