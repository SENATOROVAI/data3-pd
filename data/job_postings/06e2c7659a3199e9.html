Position Summary:
Under the general direction of the Santa Clara County 4-H Youth Development Advisor, the Community Education Specialist 3 will oversee the day to day operations of the 4-H Computer Science Pathway program, provide direction to the 4-H Community Education Specialist 2 positions and volunteers who are partners of this 4-H program. The CES 3 will administer University of California, USDA and State 4-H Office policy associated with the 4-H Youth Development Program in Santa Clara County. The CES 3 will work closely with the California 4-H Computer Science (CS) Pathway steering committee to develop, sustain, manage and grow the participation of youth and volunteers in the 4-H CS Pathway program and will work closely with 4-H advisors to help support and expand the 4-H Youth Development program in four geographic areas in California. This position will be responsible for developing projects, programs, activities and resource materials for youth and volunteer 4-H leaders and will develop and implement the computer science 4-H Youth Development programs to be integrated into communities and schools as appropriate.

Duties
Manage the California 4-H Computer Science Program that is developmentally age appropriate, research based, and consistent with UC philosophy, mission, policies and procedures. Develop projects, programs, lesson plans, activities and resource materials for experiential-based CS education for volunteers and youth. Develop and implement special CS 4-H Youth Development Programs.

Oversee the maintenance of records, enrollment, and related data necessary for carrying out the 4-H Program and prepare reports and correspondence as needed.

Promote timely communication through newsletters, emails, news releases, website, collaborative network meetings, phone calls and other informational means.

Participate in educational and training opportunities to increase personal knowledge and skills. Attend county, regional, state and national meetings and trainings as appropriate.

Attend UCCE staff meetings and work collegiality with UCCE staff.
