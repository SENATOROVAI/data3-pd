Lead Engineer, ATE Test and Characterization - Sunnyvale, CA

Minimum qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in ME, CE, CS or EE
  • 8 years experience in IC product or test engineering
  • Experience with: semiconductors, ATE hardware and wafer probe card design, managing suppliers on execution of test hardware development/bring-up, programming/scripting, probability/ statistical fundamentals, DFT, test hardware and program development, characterization, qualification, bring-up, yield improvement, debugging

Preferred qualifications:

  • Experience working with offshore test house or contract manufacturers such as ASE, SPIL, KYEC, eSilicon, TSMC, UMC
  • Experience as a technical lead engineer that has generations of product production experience
  • Hands on technical lead or leader of a small technical team
  • Presentation and communication skills to work closely with vendor/partners

About the job

Our computational challenges are so big, complex and unique we can't just purchase off-the-shelf hardware, we've got to make it ourselves. Your team designs and builds the hardware, software and networking technologies that power all of Google's services. As a Hardware Engineer, you design and build the systems that are the heart of the world's largest and most powerful computing infrastructure. You develop from the lowest levels of circuit design to large system design and see those systems all the way through to high volume manufacturing. Your work has the potential to shape the machinery that goes into our cutting-edge data centers affecting millions of Google users.

As a ATE (Automated Testing Equipment) Test and Characterization Technical Lead, you have a track record in delivering products in the fabless semiconductor model with knowledge covering the full manufacturing flow including fab, test, DFT, package assembly and qualification. You will be responsible for developing test programs and hardware for wafer probe and the ATE for characterization and production testing of high performance networking and machine learning ICs. You utilize the ATE equipment tools and capabilities to optimize final test programs without compromising the quality of outgoing products. You will also be responsible for developing test plans and supporting the full life cycle of the product from design to volume manufacturing.

Responsibilities

  • Design high performance IC test and characterization flows for PVT, signal and power integrity characterization, participate in debug, electrical failure analysis (EFA) and physical failure analysis (PFA)
  • Develop high speed Analog and Digital interface test methodologies, implement ATE test, characterization and debug programs
  • Lead test planning and execution with design, verification and DFT teams and translate concepts to product testing requirements, develop documentation, ATE HW design, and final test program development
  • Own wafer and package level test hardware and SW development and bring up offshore test house
  • Establish manufacturing requirements and specification for delivery of the product and close potential test gaps and correlations issues across the product supply chain
