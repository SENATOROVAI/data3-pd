The Instrumentation, Controls, & Data Science department within INL's Nuclear Science & Technology directorate is looking for a talented data professional to join its growing team of technical professionals.

The Data Scientist will serve as a company resource in the areas of database management, web-based data visualization, and statistical/mathematical data analysis. The Scientist will assume significant responsibility for continued development and application of INLâ€™s Nuclear Data Management and Analysis System (NDMAS) (database and web-based data interface) that supports the major nuclear research projects at INL.

Consulting with senior level scientists, engineers, and management on a variety of research projects, and applying current statistical and mathematical techniques to relevant engineering and scientific problems is vital in this role. Further, the Scientist may mentor junior members of the department in database design and management, data analysis, and in other areas of professional expertise.

Publishing results of work in peer-reviewed journals and conference proceedings will also be expected.

If you are passionate about data science, a team player, and meet or exceed the qualification below, we encourage you to apply for an opportunity to potentially join our awesome team!

QUALIFICATIONS
BS degree and 2-5 years related experience; MS degree and 1-3 years related experience; or PhD degree and 0-2 years related experience.

Strongly preferred that most recent degree is in data science, statistics, mathematics, operations research, or another associated field.

Must be able to work in a culturally diverse environment.

Proficiency with SAS software tools for data management and data analysis.

Proficiency with Structured Query Language operations, using Microsoft SQL Server Management Studio or SAS.

Ability to use SAS/SQL software to capture and store - in a relational database - diverse data sets from a wide variety of file types.

Ability to work both independently and collaboratively with professionals from different fields and backgrounds, including statisticians, mathematicians, information management engineers, nuclear research scientists, chemists, microbiologists, etc.

Strongly preferred that selected candidate has the ability to obtain and maintain a Department of Energy level Q security clearance.

Preferred knowledge and experience with non-traditional databases (e.g. Graph-,NoSQL- databases) and cloud-based database management

Preferred familiarity or experience with machine learning, artificial intelligence, or other deep learning techniques

Preferred programming skills related to development and implementation of interactive web-based data visualization tools (e.g. Java-script, Python, HTML)

Preferred familiarity with common statistical fields, such as sampling, experimental design, survival analysis, linear and non-linear regression, and time series analysis - for application to engineering issues including planning reactor experiments, assessing manufacturing quality control data, comparing treatment methods from experiments conducted in fields ranging from human factors nuclear fuel development.
