Marlin Business Services Corp. ® is a publicly traded bank holding company (NASDAQ: MRLN) that provides nationwide commercial financing and depository products. Marlin specializes in providing innovative financial solutions for small and mid-size businesses.

Marlin's value proposition is centered around providing excellent service and delivering on commitments made to equipment dealers, manufacturers, resellers, distributors and their customers.

Summary:

The Fraud Analyst will be the lead person responsible for Marlin’s fraud risk mitigation efforts, including development and implementation of leading-edge tools, processes and overall thought leadership.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Develop and implement effective fraud prevention and detection strategies to manage identity theft and application fraud losses to within Marlin’s risk appetite
  • Ensure that an appropriate balance between risk, operational costs and customer experience is maintained
  • Lead large scale projects including testing and deployment of new fraud risk scores, and development of business cases for new technology
  • Build, maintain and promulgate fraud reporting including designing KPI, Scorecards, and Dashboards
  • File SAR reports
  • Lead fraud recovery efforts, working with Legal Counsel and law enforcement
  • Perform ad-hoc analysis to identify trends, enhance fraud rules, and improve automation
  • Diagnose problems and develop compelling, data-driven recommendations, policies and processes with the goal of minimizing fraud losses
  • Maintain professional and technical knowledge by tracking emerging trends in fraud prevention, attending educational workshops; reviewing professional publications

Qualifications:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Statistics, Economics, Finance, Mathematics, Computer Science, Credit Operation or a related quantitative field, is required. An advanced degree is highly desirable
  • Professional and related certification are preferred, including: Certified Fraud Examiner (CFE), Anti Money laundering (ACAMS), Financial Crime Specialist (CFCS), Certified Credit Executive (CCE)
  • Minimum 2 years’ experience in the fraud risk discipline, preferably in the banking/financial services industry
  • Strong analytical skills with familiarity working in large and complex database environment
  • Highly organized and detail oriented with the proven ability to multi-task
  • Knowledge and use of external open data sources and ability to leverage same with internal signal and data sources
  • Excellent interpersonal skills, including verbal, written and presentation communication skills
  • Excellent problem solving, conflict resolution and change management skills
  • Self-motivated, with ability to actively lead and implement ideas effectively in a collaborative and cross-functional working environment
  • Fraud detection data and other related application use a plus including: commercial and consumer credit bureaus, social network analysis, domain tools, device-fingerprinting etc
