We have an exciting opportunity for a Supplier Development Quality Engineer Sr. to support our Supply Chain Team. This position is located in Kalamazoo, Michigan.

Major Duties

1. Monitors delivery and quality performance, trends and manufacturing process improvements by coordinating with suppliers and verifying objective evidence. Applies problem solving techniques, to develop supplier programs to improve supplier performance and reduce risk. 2. Asses supplier capacity Business Health and Production Readiness. Determines supplier succession planning, growth potential and operational sustainability. 3. Refines and enhances products and processes by applying innovative continuous improvement and key lean manufacturing/production principles and techniques to critical areas of a supplier’s production (PCI). Grow and evolve the Continuous Improvement POL Database as a source of best practices. 4. Provides input on supplier capability, delivery and quality to product development teams; recommends producibility improvements by facilitating cross functional process reviews, addressing technical data packages, manufacturing methods, equipment, tooling and training at the supplier. 5. Initiate and train supplier in the PPAP process. Understands statistical process controls by reviewing and maintaining applicable supporting documents (DFMEA, PFMEA, Flow Charts, Control plans. Analyzes data by completing hypothesis, normal distribution, and process capability analysis tests (Minitab). Six-Sigma project portfolio management. 6. Assesses capability, delivery and quality performance (or cost-of-quality) using statistical and analytical methods. Utilizes A3 problem solving tool to drive supplier performance plans. Maintains status on actions and supports Go-To-Green process. 7. Represent Parker on supplier development projects. Contributes to team effort by accomplishing related results as needed. Work assignments may include cross-functional or project team responsibilities (e.g. continuous improvement). Develops project goals and objectives to complete complex or major supplier engineering projects by reviewing quality performance trends, statistical plans, cost estimates, quality plans, and conferring with management. Ensures project meets schedule, cost results, and technical expectations through effective project leadership. 8. Determines supplier engineering project responsibilities by identifying project phases and elements, assigning staffing and resources, and reviewing bids from outside contractors. 9. Determines project schedule by studying project plan and specifications; calculating time requirements; sequencing project elements. Maintains project schedule by monitoring project progress, coordinating activities, and resolving problems. 10. Controls project plan by reviewing design specifications, and plan or schedule changes, and recommending actions to achieve quality and customer service objectives. Controls project costs by approving expenditures and administering contractor contracts. 11. Applies and leads continuous improvement, cost savings and manufacturing process focused initiatives for critical areas of production. 12. Interprets regulations, contracts, and company policy on areas applying to quality measures and standards. Complies with federal, state, and local legal requirements by studying existing and new legislation, ensuring adherence to requirements, and advising management on needed actions. 13. Recommends actions by analyzing and interpreting data and making comparative analyses. Analyzes proposed changes in methods and materials for cost and quality impacts. 14. Supports supplier work transfers, performs supplier capability analysis to support the source selection process, provides knowledge transfer to new suppliers and monitors the development of new processes and products at the supplier. Identifies risks and assists suppliers during the transition phase of the transfer. 15. Maintains professional and technical knowledge by attending educational workshops, benchmarking state-of-the-art practices, reviewing publications, and/or participating in professional associations. 16. Shares specialized knowledge with others. Represents company on specific projects. Interfaces with all agencies and customers as necessary to complete projects. Contributes to team effort by accomplishing related results as needed.

Responsibilities

Develops, grows and communicates a functional understanding of Parker products and services sold with our suppliers and customers. Influences supplier’s behavior and commitment to quality, delivery and cost to supply the end user with a premiere customer experience. Works closely with suppliers to develop their capability to produce Parker products with repeatable and sustainable processes to ensure zero defects and on time delivery to need dates. Utilizes PMP, Continuous Improvement, Six-Sigma, APQP and PPAP methods in everyday activities. Builds a culture of Premier Supplier Performance innovation and strategic supplier partnerships. Hosts and supports supplier Kaizen events conferences and symposiums. Generates and applies LEAN, Problem Solving and Innovative ideas to suppliers and the Supplier Development Organization. Ensures timely and efficient completion of detailed supplier engineering project phases or major projects to achieve division quality objectives. Plans, schedules, and coordinates efforts of the supplier development engineering staff or acts as an individual researcher or functional expert on projects with significant customers/suppliers and business impact. Identifies supplier risks and work on mitigation plans with the suppliers to eliminate identified risks. Performs VAVE events with the suppliers to identify opportunities to reduce manufacturing costs. Establish and grow supplier partnerships through training of contract requirements and customer expectations. Communicates supplier capabilities back to internal teams to assist in work transfer, long term agreements and cost reduction activities.
