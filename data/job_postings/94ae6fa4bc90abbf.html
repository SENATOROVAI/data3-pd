Personal Care Assistant - Yuba City, CA

KNOWLEDGE:
High school graduate or equivalent required

SKILLS:
Good written and organizational skills. Demonstrate ability to set priorities and organize tasks in a timely manner. Ability to work independently in a fast-paced and rapid changing environment

EXPERIENCE:
One year experience in assisted living facility preferred.

LICENSE:
NA

DEFINITION: The Personal Care Assistant (PCA) performs routine duties, in caring for residents which are supportive and complementary to nursing practice.

RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Actively participates in the resident's plan of care and assists in implementing care intervention at residents level of care
  • Upon resident admission, assists licensed personnel with data collection, through basic physical appearance, vital signs, weights, and documents accordingly. Continues basic assessments during routine resident care on a daily basis, reporting change of condition to Facility Director/Physician/and family members in a timely manner
  • Charts clearly and concisely in a timely manner, according to policy and procedure
  • Provides a clean and safe environment for all residents. Assists residents to their individual level of capability, their cleanliness, grooming, nourishments, rest, activity and elimination. Ensure teeth brushing daily
  • Demonstrates ability to assess emergency situations and take appropriate action
  • Reports to physician and resident's responsible party change in condition in a timely manner and performs all necessary documentation. This includes calling families when a resident falls
  • As assigned, completes any resident care responsibilities or assignments left from previous shift and reports any incomplete assignments to the next shift
  • Makes routine rounds of assigned area and report pertinent information to Facility Director or Assisted Living Manager throughout the shift, also charts accordingly
  • Makes resident rounds at beginning and end of shift relaying pertinent information and fulfilling resident needs. At the end of shift, also gives verbal report to on-coming staff while making rounds
  • Strip and make residents' beds as often as needed. Launder resident's linen and towels, fold and return to residents
  • Assist in serving meals and bussing tables during mealtimes
  • Assist with the activity program; conduct activities as directed by the Activity Coordinator or assigned by supervisor when needed
  • Document information pertaining to changes in residents' functional status, personal needs, appointments, etc
  • Provide one-on-one support/visitation to assigned residents on a weekly basis or as requested by supervisor or as specified in the assistance plan or daily communication log
  • Identify supplies needed to perform tasks and report needs to supervisor
  • Practice safety in working around and with equipment, chemicals, tools and utensils. Using the buddy system with a challenging resident
  • Carry out other duties as assigned by supervisor
  • Acknowledges and assists others promptly and courteously, refers to other departments as appropriate, and demonstrates compassion allowing time for residents and significant others to verbalize
  • Performs other duties as assigned.
