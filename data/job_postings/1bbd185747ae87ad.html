Autonomous Driving Engineer – Advanced Tracking and Sensor Fusion - Dearborn, MI 48126

Candidates for consideration will be responsible for supporting research and developmental efforts in automated vehicle technologies ranging from Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) to semi- and fully-autonomous driving capabilities.

Responsibilities
  • Design and implement algorithms for environmental perception, including multi-object tracking, classification, and sensor fusion using heterogeneous sensors (e.g. radar, camera, LIDAR and ultrasonic)
  • Algorithm/code optimization for real-time feasibility and implementation in the production target hardware
  • Support the sensor and algorithm calibration, testing, interface specification to support multiple SAE level 2/3 features
  • Develop, implement and utilize appropriate metrics to quantify the performance of advanced tracking and fusion algorithms
  • Design and execution of experiments, data collections and analysis, and performance evaluation from both real sensor data and realistically simulated data for complex use cases
  • Presentation of requirements, designs, implementation details and results during technical review meetings
Basic Qualifications
  • One of the following:
    • Master’s Degree or PhD in Physics, Mathematics, Robotics, Electrical Engineering, Signal and/or Data Processing, Computer Science, or related field, WITH 3+ years of experience in degree field
OR
  • Bachelor’s Degree in Physics, Mathematics, Robotics, Electrical Engineering, Signal and/or Data Processing, Computer Science, or related field, WITH 5+ years of experience in degree field
AND
  • 5+ years of experience with software development in C/C++ or MATLAB (graduate level course work allowed)
  • 3+ years of experience with algorithm development and implementation in the field of target/object tracking and/or sensor fusion
Preferred Qualifications
  • 3+ years of algorithm development experience in the automotive industry, particularly, autonomous (SAE Level 2 and up) vehicle systems
  • 3+ years of experience in RADAR tracker algorithm development
  • Strong knowledge of Bayesian estimation theory, non-linear filtering, object detection/classification, multi-sensor multi-target tracking and fusion methods, multiple motion modeling, sensor modeling, motion and measurement noise characterization
  • Good knowledge of Machine-Learning principles and approaches
  • Experience in working in an agile software development environment and tools such as Git, Jenkins, Jira, etc
  • Experience with CAN, UDP, TCP, and network routing systems
  • Experience with sensor systems such as LIDAR, radar, cameras, etc
  • Strong foundation in linear algebra and numerical methods, vector analysis, and probability/statistics
  • Experience in Failure Mode Effect Analysis (FMEA) activities
  • Proficiency in multiple operating systems such as Windows, UNIX, Linux, QNX, etc
  • Experience with other contemporary languages such as PERL, Python, Java, OpenGL, etc
