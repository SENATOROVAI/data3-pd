Clinical Data Engineer - United States

REPORTS TO

Director, Data Sciences

SUMMARY OF POSITION

The Corrona Data Sciences & Engineering group is seeking a [clinical] data engineer to support data integration and develop and maintain data processing systems and pipelines to support the data science project lifecycle, as well as Corrona enterprise analytics and integrity. The Data Engineer will support to all aspects of Data Science’s mission-including data/analytical management, ETL, EDA, machine learning methods, governance and operations-for multiple structured/unstructured sources of longitudinal health information, with the goal of widening the breadth of applications while increasing the fidelity of our models in an automatable fashion.

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

  • Integration: Ingest, transform, integrate and deliver structured and unstructured observational and controlled clinical/business data to a scalable data warehouse platform
  • Engineering: Develop and maintain scalable data processing systems and pipelines for preparing data from various sources for analytical modeling.
    • Learn and maintain the source/domain data model for storing clinical data
    • Develop, construct, test and maintain the analytical data infrastructure
    • Implement, test and maintain clinical quality measures and outcomes assessments
    • Write high-quality, documented, well-tested, readable, standardized, maintainable, and versioned code
    • Deliver in a highly agile, cross-functional environment
  • Visualization: Contribute in design, development and deployment of sustainable and scalable data visualizations and analytics based on input, consultation and requirements from team members and stakeholders
  • Data Governance: Collaborate in the overall management of the availability, standardization, documentation, transparency, operations, architecture and integrity of enterprise data
  • Generalist/Full stack: Competencies across several different fields, methods, applications, etc. that will support the data engineering and science project lifecycle. Introducing new technologies and solutions to the data engineering processes that will facilitate machine learning at a higher scale

EDUCATION & EXPERIENCE

Required:

  • Master’s Degree with a least 4 years’ experience in data engineering with a concentration in mathematics, statistics, probability, computer engineering/science and machine learning methods
  • Previous experience working within a clinical, pharmaceutical, and healthcare data industry. Omics data a plus, but not decision criteria as preference is on ClinROs and PROs
  • Knowledge of data methods and procedures with minimum 3 years of programming experience in Python
  • 2+ years of experience and proficiency in delivering SQL-based solutions to satisfy detailed application requirements, including strong understanding of data normalization
  • Experience with cloud platform infrastructure; in particular, Amazon Web Services (AWS)
  • Demonstrated ability to organize and integrate complex systems efficiently and effectively, with a strong comprehension of data structures, algorithms and performance optimization
  • Experience using Git, svn, or other source control systems
  • Experience handling both structured and unstructured data from disparate systems, and complicated longitudinal datasets
  • Work independently with little supervision and a driven spirit and can remain upbeat and creative in a challenging legacy business model

Preferred:

  • Experience with programming in Java
  • Translation of legacy systems and applications into rules/definition-based applications
  • Experience with data analytics platforms, frameworks and/or workflow tools

Other:

  • Must be highly organized, initiative driven and detail-oriented, with excellent time management skills
  • Must possess strong English communication and writing skills and be able to work independently and as part of a team
  • Completion of Good Clinical Practice (GCP) training (provided by Corrona)

WORK ENVIRONMENT

  • Remote position with preference given to candidates in the Baltimore or Boston Metro areas
  • Must be able to remain in a stationary position 50%
  • Travel within the US - up to 15%
