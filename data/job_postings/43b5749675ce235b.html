The Role

Summary of Position
The field scout position works with the Pest Control Department and, in particular, the Pest Control Adviser (PCA). The primary duties are to patrol the orchards and provide information from the field to the PCA. This information is crucial to the PCA in forming written recommendations for crop protection and nutrient programs.
Key Responsibilities
Monitor and analyze orchards for insects and disease infestation.
Assess the overall appearance of the orchards, and then examine specific plants including buds, stems, leaves and roots.
Examine insect traps and spider mites routinely. Bug sampling using beating trays and sweep nets. Inspect and map weed populations.
Inform PCA of any problems or irregularities in the fields being monitored.
Sample crop quality and transport leaf tissue, hull, and any other related requests for detailed analysis.
Assist on research projects (as available).
Oversee crews, including spray crews, when directed by the PCA or Crop Managers.
Detect problems that will affect future crops.
Skills
Specific knowledge required to satisfactorily perform the functions of the job includes: Ability to make short- and long-term pest management decisions.
Strong grasp on how to watch for typical damage symptoms that are caused by pests and pesticides.
Knowledge of insect, mite, and weed identification.
Familiar with life stages of crops as well as pest insects and weeds.
Understanding on conducting tests and inspections of products, services, or processes to
evaluate quality or performance.
Understanding the implications of new information for both current and future problem- solving and decision-making.
Ability to organize, write and file field data.
