POSITION: RN Provider (Part-Time)


REPORTS TO: Regional Manager - Operations


DESCRIPTION, BACKGROUND AND OBJECTIVES:


Just as the Physicians of VCA develop, perfect and focus their attention on the treatment of large vein disease through the utilization of image guided injections or endovenous laser treatment, RN Providers specialize in the treatment of medium and small vein disease utilizing visually guided sclerotherapy under the clinical supervision of the Physician. Working together, the Physician and the RN Provider are able to provide complete and highly efficacious treatment to patients presenting with a combination of large vein disease and medium/small vein disease.


Specialization also affords RN Providers with the opportunity to work towards and build semi-independent practices with patients that present with just medium and small vein disease.


The RN Providers are integral to both clinical and practice building operations. While RN Providers, as well as the physicians, are supported by a strong operational team at both the clinic and the Home Office that provide a multitude of services ranging from marketing to billing and collections to patient financial planning to patient services, they also participate, as do the Physicians, in local practice building activities. These activities include participation in the Referring MD programs designed to create referrals from other physician practices and managing and maintaining long-term relations with their own patient base in order to retain existing patients and create referrals from friends and family members of existing patients. All of these activities benefit the clinic practice generally and their own practices in particular.


With the benefits of creating and managing a semi-independent practice also come the responsibilities inherent in any mutually supportive team effort.


RESPONSIBILITIES:


At the direction of the Physician, assist the Physician in treating all patients, and at the direction of the Physician, maintain a separate schedule specifically for the treatment of Medium and Small Vein Disease. RN Providers are responsible for maintaining clinic inventory of medical supplies (including prepared solution), and are expected to assist the clinic team in practice building and administrative activities when possible.


DUTIES:

The duties of the RN Provider fall into four equally important categories that are integral to the success of the practice:

Clinical Support

  • Assist the Physician with treatment of all patients including ELT, Sclerotherapy, Trapped Blood and Ulcer
  • Complete and ensure that, “Before and After” photographs have been taken for all patients, and photos are properly printed and organized into the appropriate patient file
  • Assist the Physician by thoroughly educating the patients on their treatment protocol as well as their responsibilities and possible complications. Secure patient signatures on consent forms once the education process has been completed
  • Take an active role in meeting with consults to explain your treatment protocol as well as providing them with an accurate estimate of MVD treatments required to fully satisfy their varicose vein disease concerns. Consultation should involve the use of the utilization of the vein light to demonstrate to the patient what veins you will be treating
  • Maintain an adequate supply of solutions and filled syringes as required by the clinic
  • Maintain the Emergency Drug Box according to policy
  • Order and maintain proper inventory of all medical supplies
  • Ensure that each exam room is stocked and cleaned daily as required
  • With the Physician, respond to patient telephone calls regarding medical conditions and emergencies
  • With the Physician, contact all patients within twenty-four hours of their initial treatment to monitor their medical condition and satisfaction level

Practice Building

  • Assist the Physician in the RMD marketing program and attend all LNLs
  • In conjunction with administrative staff, develop and maintain relationships with other practices, physicians and nurses to set up LNL presentations and encourage referrals
  • Maintain contact with existing MVD patients to ensure a long-term relationship, the completion of treatment and compliance with periodic re-evaluations
  • As needed, assist the administrative staff in making follow up contact with all patients including non-convert consults
  • Solicit Friend and Family Referrals during all visits with patients
  • Participate in all clinic practice building activities including conversions of patients – first and second legs

Administrative Support

  • Monitor and enforce all OSHA policies and guidelines as directed by the Home Office
  • Take the active role as Laser Safety Officer for your clinic and ensure that all standards are being maintained as per VCA’s Laser Safety Policy
  • As needed, assist the administrative staff with such functions such as data entry, appointment scheduling, chart reviews, patient check-in and checkout and filing

Practice Management and Treatment

  • At the direction of the Physician, schedule and treat MVD/SVD patients independently of the Physician’s schedule
  • Participate in the conversion off all initials and second leg starts
  • Educate the patient on the importance of compliance relative to Stage II and III appointments
  • Maintain schedule to maximize the number of treatment slots required to meet patient demand and to fulfill other duties
  • Maintain the highest skill level for the safest and most effective delivery of treatment according to accepted VCA clinical guidelines
  • Provide the highest level of customer service to promote patient satisfaction

COMPLIANCE:

  • Adhere to both the spirit and the language of the Sarbanes–Oxley Act of 2002 (“SOX”) in order to avoid any conduct that would be in violation of SOX or give the appearance of violating SOX
  • Understand SOX rules under the Company’s compliance policies and procedures, and the penalties for not complying with the policies and procedures and the law
