Senior Research Associate - San Antonio, TX 78230

The Senior Research Associate works with clients and internal staff on projects involving the gathering of data, conducting data analysis, and meeting time-sensitive delivery goals. The primary requirement for this role is a solid training and demonstrated expertise in quantitative fields, such as finance, economics, statistics, operations research, and/or business analytics.
Candidates should have expertise in theoretical and applied econometrics, plus specializations in one or more of these areas: financial economics, labor economics, and risk management. This position requires excellent analytic, technical writing, and presentation skills.
Job Responsibilities
  • Under the supervision of the Director, directs the design, research, development, and delivery of statistical products, projects, and enhancements to address business needs
  • Supports in the development of project requirements, analytic plans, project timelines, and related materials for the management of deliverables
  • Serves as project team lead and client point of contact, as needed
  • Keeps abreast of new and emerging statistical and data analysis techniques
  • Provides guidance on advanced statistical methodologies to team members
  • Supports the Director in supervisory duties for research associates, as needed
  • Explores and capitalizes on opportunities to cross-sell and up-sell existing clients
  • Supports the sales process by assessing prospective clients’ needs and crafting and presenting proposals that meet those needs
  • Additional responsibilities as assigned
Job Requirements
  • Master’s degree in Statistics, Applied Mathematics or related field, Ph.D. preferred
  • Minimum of 3 years of consulting experience in Statistics, Finance, Economics, and/or Applied Mathematics
  • Solid planning, priority setting, and project management skills with experience managing multiple projects and resources concurrently
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills
  • Excellent knowledge of various statistical methodologies such as regression analysis (linear and non-linear), cluster analysis, CHAID, factor analysis/principal component analysis, time series, survival models
  • Strong programming skills using various statistical packages, such as SAS, SPSS, Stata, R, etc
  • Ability to display data visually, creating powerful presentations which effectively demonstrate the value of analytic deliverables
  • Highly motivated, collaborative, and innovative
  • Strong technical skills
Performance Expectations
  • Employee meets project milestones/deadlines
  • Employee produces a quality work product that is accurate and free from careless errors
  • Employee is a contributing and dependable member of project teams
  • Employee diligently and accurately records project time billed to clients
  • Employee completes supervisory duties in a timely manner
