Epidemiologist Coordinator - San Francisco, CA

The San Francisco Department of Public Health (SFDPH) Population Health Division (PHD) Applied Research, Community Health Epidemiology, and Surveillance (ARCHES) Branch is seeking an Epidemiologist Coordinator to support the Strengthening the U.S. Response to Resistant Gonorrhea (SURRG) project, a California Bay Area collaborative effort with the California Department of Public Health (CDPH). The SURRG Epidemiologist Coordinator will primarily serve as the lead analyst and project manager for a multi-site CDC initiative on rapidly detecting and responding to antibiotic resistant gonorrhea (ARG), as well as support other STD surveillance and evaluation activities.

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

Under the supervision of the SFDPH SURRG Project Lead/STD Epidemiologist, the Epidemiologist Coordinator will perform the following essential job functions:

  • Serve as primary liaison to all project partners including: CDC, California Department of Public Health (CDPH) STD Branch, SF Public Health Laboratory, and participating STD and other care clinics;
  • Apply strong analytical skills and knowledge using statistical and database management software (i.e., SAS) to conduct quality assurance of all related clinical, laboratory, and investigation data; and conduct evaluation of the epidemiologic investigation data to provide quality improvement feedback to staff performing interviews and partner services;
  • Conduct epidemiologic and network analyses using clinical, laboratory, and investigation data to describe patients with and without ARG and the sexual and social networks in which they are located;
  • Evaluate data using descriptive and multivariate statistics as part of verifying, analyzing, interpreting, and summarizing data for reports, as well as for dissemination of findings to appropriate stakeholders;
  • Develop and maintain protocols and workflows to meet project deliverables related to specimen collection at participating clinics, antibiotic resistance testing at the SF Public Health Laboratory, and coordination of clinical follow-up of SURRG cases/contacts identified at non-STD clinics;
  • Coordinate meetings and trainings and conduct related follow-up to meet project deliverables and ensure involvement by key staff;
  • Work collaboratively with the project’s CDPH Regional Epidemiology Coordinator whose primary duties include oversight of the interjurisdictional and SF disease investigation and communication protocols, project budget and purchasing, and development of a Bay Area ARG response plan; and serving as the primary liaison to the Bay Area county health departments and supervisor of the SURRG Regional Health Worker;
  • Respond to ad-hoc data requests by CDC, CDPH, or other SFDPH partners including external researchers and the public;
  • Participate on project conference calls and attend required trainings and meetings as needed;
  • Effectively apply communication skills to translate data to program and clinic staff, and community members;
  • Prepare project progress reports and continuing grant applications for submission to funding agencies;
  • Summarize study data for presentation and publication in technical and scientific journals and at local and national meetings;
  • Maintain a detailed and well-organized schedule of tasks and events;
  • Meet with supervisor weekly and as-needed to report progress and solve problems;
    Respond creatively and professionally to challenges that arise in the course of the project;
  • Address additional SFDPH PHD STD epidemiological needs as requested;
  • Assure ethical practices and maintain confidentiality;
  • Ensure all patients’ rights are protected;
  • Performs other related duties as assigned/required

JOB QUALIFICATIONS

Education/Experience

  • Master of Public Health or Master of Science in Epidemiology, public health, infectious diseases, or related field, required
  • 2 or more years’ experience in epidemiology and/or analyzing surveillance, health research, or healthcare data
  • Minimum of one year experience with database management and quality assurance, preferred
  • Minimum of one year experience coordinating public health projects or research studies, preferred
  • Basic content knowledge of STD epidemiology and prevention and control strategies, preferred

Certificates/Licenses/Clearances

  • None required

Other Skills, Knowledge, and Abilities

  • Knowledge of STD and HIV and medical terminology
  • Knowledge of the principles and procedures of epidemiological research including study design, epidemiological surveys, and surveillance; theory and application of epidemiological methodologies; principles and method of biostatistics, and preparation of scientific research reports
  • Knowledge and skills to conduct social network analysis of cases and their reported sexual partners and social contacts
  • Strong SQL skills for statistical and database management software in addition to SAS; knowledge of STATA or R is a plus
  • Strong oral and written communication and data visualization skills
  • Exceptional interpersonal skills
  • Leadership, organization, and decision-making skills
  • Work independently as well as part of a team
  • Prioritize and handle multiple tasks
  • Sensitivity to and experience working with ethnically, culturally and sexually diverse individuals, communities, agencies and organizations that comprise the STD/HIV public health constituency
