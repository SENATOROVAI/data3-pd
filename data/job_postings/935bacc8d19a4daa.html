We have an opening for a Chemistry Material Science Technologist to work in the High Explosives Application Facility (HEAF) within the Energetic Materials Center (EMC). Under general supervision the selected candidate will collaborate with principal investigators, and scientific and technical staff to provide technical support in the design, development and execution of scientific experiments. You will be required to provide solutions to general scientific problems in the areas of chemistry and material science. This position is in the Reaction Dynamics Group in the Materials Science Division (MSD).

This position will be filled at either a 504.2 or 504.3 level depending on your qualifications. Additional job responsibilities (outlined below) will be assigned if you are selected at the higher level.

Essential Duties
  • Partner with scientists, engineers and high-level technical personnel to provide general technical support to various programmatic tasks
  • Ability to acquire and maintain the necessary qualifications as an Explosive Hander
  • Contribute to explosives research by providing general support for explosive characterization activities
  • Ensure effective operation and maintenance of instrumentation systems by following quality control requirements. Schedule and monitor vendor maintenance of instrumentation systems
  • Ability to acquire and analyze basic data and keep laboratory notebook, prepare data summaries of results and identify problems
  • Ability to lift up to 50 lbs. in a safe and controlled manner, climb a ladder, and wear a respirator as needed
  • Perform other duties as assigned
In Addition, at the 504.3 Level
  • Provide chemistry knowledge and advanced technical support to resolve complex technical and programmatic issues
  • Prepare reports, identify problems, and suggest solutions to scientific and technical staff
  • Establish or modify guideline techniques and procedures, to choose appropriate work methods, and set priorities
Qualifications
  • Associate degree in chemistry, materials science or environmental technology or the equivalent combination of education and related experience
  • Elementary understanding of chemistry and materials science
  • Experience working with an experimental team and working effectively under conflicting and rigorous deadlines with general supervision
  • Handling and general knowledge of chemicals in laboratory settings
  • Willingness to work with explosives, which requires the ability to deal with hazards inherent in explosives research in a responsible and efficient manner
  • Organizational and interpersonal skills to work with experimental teams that include members from all disciplines
  • Ability to work in a collaborative research and development environment
  • Proficient verbal and written communication skills, to present results verbally and through written reports
In Addition, at the 504.3 Level
  • Advanced knowledge or unique skills in one or more scientific techniques in the physical sciences or explosives
  • Experience working in explosives processing facilities which have a high degree of administrative control (e.g., DOE explosive firing/processing/handling facilities)
Desired Qualifications
  • Knowledge of LLNL programs in energetic materials and LLNL ES&H rules and regulations
  • Experience in operation of advanced scientific analytical instrumentation and use of commercial instrumentation software
  • Experience and technical knowledge in the installation, operation, and maintenance of chemical instrumentation


Pre-Placement Medical Exam: A job related pre-placement medical examination may be required.
