Scientific Engineering Associate - San Francisco Bay Area, CA

Berkeley Lab’s Material Sciences Division has an exciting opening for a Scientific Engineering Associate position. In this role you will provide multi-functional technical and operational support at a EUV photolithography materials research center used to advance the state-of-the-art in computer chip manufacturing capabilities. This is a unique opportunity to join a group of dynamic individuals and use the world’s highest resolution projection lithography tool to explore the forefront of science and technology.

What You Will Do:
  • Operate, troubleshoot, and maintain:
    • EUV lithography exposure systems, synchrotron beamline instrumentation (gratings, detectors, apertures, pumps, valves), commercial semiconductor processing equipment (prime, spin-coat, develop hot plate), vacuum systems, optical microscopes, scanning electron microscopes, and ellipsometers
    • commercial semiconductor wafer processing track
    • commercial semiconductor processing equipment (prime, spin-coat, develop hot plate),
    • vacuum systems,
    • optical microscopes,
    • scanning electron microscopes,
    • ellipsometers / film thickness measurement systems
  • Synthesize, handle, and dispose of chemicals; clean and dry chemical glassware
  • Ship, receive, purchase, and manage inventory of chemicals and consumables (wafers, wafer containers, chemicals, and personal protective equipment)
  • Create many unique experimental samples using chemicals and commercial semiconductor processing equipment / track, each matching a unique specified recipe/prescription, with infrequent error
  • Acquire scanning electron microscope (SEM) images of patterned photoresist samples. Use SEM images to provide accurate feedback to the operator of a photolithography instrument on how well focused and exposed the patterned samples are
  • Analyze data, uncover trends in data, and makes sound, data-based decisions
  • Provide exemplary customer service to our international scientific user community
  • With the assistance of Scientific Staff, guide and support onsite/offsite Users to successfully accomplish scientific objectives and explains experimental data
  • Train co-workers, users, customers about related instrumentation, concepts, processes, and methods
  • Identifies anomalous experimental data and anomalous instrument behavior and communicates information to scientific staff
  • Refine processes and techniques to improve efficiency, quality, and reduce waste or cost
  • Collect and monitor data related to equipment and systems performance
  • Maintain positive, friendly, can-do environment especially when interacting with customers, co-workers, and members of management

What is Required:
  • Five years of work experience in a functional specialty closely aligned with the relevant program
  • Knowledge equivalent to a Bachelor’s Degree in a functional specialty closely aligned with the relevant program
  • Proficiency in the areas of operation of scientific/technical instrumentation, written and oral scientific/technical communication, process-oriented tasks (manufacturing, synthesis, or testing)
  • Ability to multitask and function well in a fast-paced work environment with distractions
  • Strong customer service skills
  • Strong data organization and analysis skills (either in Excel, MATLAB, or equivalent)
  • Strong written, oral, and interpersonal communication skills
  • Strong analytical, critical thinking, judgement, and problem-solving skills
  • Strong technical and personal references
  • Familiar with Linux, Mac, and Windows operating systems
  • Familiar with Word and Excel

Additional Desired Qualifications:
  • Working knowledge of photolithography exposure systems
  • Working knowledge of college level optics concepts – diffraction gratings, zone plates, lenses, coatings, diffraction, interference, imaging equation
  • Experience working with chemicals and familiarity with basic chemistry techniques and equipment
  • Practical knowledge of semiconductor processing systems including spin coaters, bake plates, ellipsometers, tracks
  • Practical / working knowledge of amateur photography concepts, i.e., numerical aperture, magnification, depth of field, depth of focus, f#, aperture stops, enlarging, development, sensitivity, etc
  • Experience in a darkroom photography lab

The University of California manages Berkeley Lab for the U.S. Department of Energy’s Office of Science.
